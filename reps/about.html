About REPS

If you've stumbled across this by chance, or you are some lucky soul who has decided to help me in testing, you might be wondering what reps actually is. The official name of the application as it stands now is REPS - Exercise Management, and, as the name implies, is an online application to aid in managing your regular exercise routine, whatever that may be. It is also intended to help people get started with a routine, if they did not previously have one, and to stay on track. The intent is to start with very basic functionality and build on it. This application is in pre-alpha stages (for non-nerds that means that it's still under heavy and constant development), and as such, you should not expect things to work perfectly.

With that, let me outline the basic functionality that exists as it currently stands:

  • Users can create workouts, which consist of one or many exercises (currently only repetition exercises are supported, however support for any and all exercises will be added).
  • Users can print out workout sheets, so that they can record their workouts while they are at the gym, or wherever it is they workout.
  • Users can enter their workout data into REPS, which can be used to track their progress.

Keep in mind, this is only the very basic functionality, which once perfected, will be built upon. As someone using REPS in its current state, I kindly ask that you make suggestions and give feedback regarding the current processes outlined above. I would like to perfect these before moving on to more advanced functionality. You can e-mail this feedback to me at reps.em@gmail.com.

If you're wondering what future functionality I have in mind, I have outlined it below:

  • Charting of progress using user defined criteria, including weight loss, weight gain, strength gain, increases in endurance, etc.
  • Ability to compare and contrast the effectiveness of particular routines by comparing progress made using one routine vs. another.
  • The ability to see which muscle groups your workouts are targeting, and the intensity you are placing on these various muscle groups.
  • A social networking aspect, albeit limited to keep the application theme on track.
